# PR Best Practices That Actually Work in 2025

**Author:** Christopher Lembke, Founder of UpsurgeIQ  
**Published:** December 21, 2025  
**Reading Time:** 8 minutes  
**Category:** Public Relations, Best Practices

---

Public relations has evolved dramatically over the past decade, yet many businesses still approach it with outdated strategies that yield minimal results. The landscape has shifted from traditional press release distribution to a sophisticated ecosystem where authentic storytelling, data-driven insights, and strategic timing determine success. Drawing from years of experience helping businesses amplify their voice across media channels, this guide distills the essential practices that separate effective PR campaigns from those that disappear into the void.

## Understanding Modern Media Landscapes

The relationship between businesses and journalists has fundamentally changed. Newsrooms operate with smaller teams, tighter deadlines, and an overwhelming influx of pitches competing for attention. Journalists receive hundreds of press releases daily, most of which fail to address their specific beat or provide genuine news value. Understanding this reality forms the foundation of effective PR strategy.

Modern PR professionals must recognize that journalists are not simply gatekeepers waiting to publish your content—they are storytellers seeking narratives that resonate with their audience. Your role is to provide them with compelling, newsworthy material that aligns with their editorial focus while saving them research time. This means thoroughly researching each publication's recent coverage, understanding their audience demographics, and tailoring your pitch to fit seamlessly into their content strategy.

The most successful PR campaigns begin with comprehensive media research. Before crafting a single press release, invest time in identifying publications whose audience aligns with your target market. Analyze their recent articles to understand tone, preferred story angles, and content gaps your business can fill. This groundwork transforms generic mass distribution into strategic, targeted outreach that respects journalists' time and increases your chances of coverage.

## Crafting Newsworthy Content

Newsworthiness remains the cornerstone of successful PR, yet many businesses struggle to identify what truly qualifies as news. A new product launch, company milestone, or service expansion may feel significant internally, but journalists evaluate stories through a different lens. They ask: Does this impact their readers? Does it reveal a broader trend? Does it challenge conventional wisdom or provide unique insights?

The strongest press releases answer the fundamental question journalists constantly ask themselves: "Why should my audience care about this right now?" Effective PR content connects your announcement to larger industry trends, societal shifts, or pressing challenges your audience faces. For example, rather than simply announcing a new software feature, frame it within the context of evolving remote work challenges or emerging data privacy concerns that your solution addresses.

Data substantiates your narrative and provides journalists with credible material to reference. Original research, proprietary surveys, or analysis of industry trends positions your business as a thought leader while giving reporters concrete statistics to anchor their stories. When possible, commission studies that reveal surprising insights about your industry—these findings often generate coverage independent of your core business announcement.

Timing amplifies newsworthiness. Align your announcements with relevant industry events, seasonal trends, or current news cycles to increase relevance. A cybersecurity company announcing new features during National Cybersecurity Awareness Month, or a financial services firm releasing economic insights ahead of quarterly earnings season, demonstrates strategic awareness that journalists appreciate.

## Building Authentic Media Relationships

The transactional approach to media relations—reaching out only when you need coverage—undermines long-term PR success. Journalists remember businesses that treat them as content distribution channels rather than professional partners. Building authentic relationships requires consistent engagement, mutual respect, and genuine value exchange that extends beyond your immediate PR needs.

Start by becoming a reliable resource. When journalists cover your industry, offer expert commentary even when it doesn't directly promote your business. Respond promptly to media inquiries, provide background information that helps them understand complex topics, and connect them with other industry experts when appropriate. This generosity builds goodwill that pays dividends when you eventually pitch your own stories.

Personalization demonstrates respect for journalists' expertise and time. Generic mass emails signal that you haven't invested effort in understanding their work, making it easy for them to delete your pitch without reading. Reference specific articles they've written, explain why your story aligns with their beat, and acknowledge their unique perspective. This approach requires more time but generates significantly higher response rates.

Social media provides opportunities for low-pressure relationship building. Follow journalists who cover your industry, engage thoughtfully with their content, and share their articles when relevant. This visibility keeps your name familiar without the pressure of a formal pitch, making future outreach feel like a natural extension of an existing professional relationship rather than cold contact.

## Leveraging Multi-Channel Distribution

Traditional press release distribution through wire services still holds value, but modern PR demands a multi-channel approach that meets journalists where they consume information. Email remains the primary channel for formal pitches, yet social media, professional networks, and direct messaging platforms increasingly influence how stories gain traction.

Email pitches require surgical precision. Subject lines must immediately communicate news value without resorting to clickbait tactics that erode credibility. The opening paragraph should deliver your core message concisely—journalists often decide whether to continue reading within the first two sentences. Include all essential information early, as many reporters skim rather than read thoroughly.

Social media amplifies your message beyond traditional media channels. Share your press release across LinkedIn, Twitter, and industry-specific platforms where your target audience and relevant journalists are active. Use platform-specific best practices: LinkedIn favors professional insights and thought leadership, while Twitter rewards concise, timely commentary on trending topics. Tag relevant publications and journalists appropriately, but avoid excessive tagging that feels spammy.

Owned media channels—your blog, newsletter, and social platforms—allow you to control your narrative while building an audience independent of traditional media coverage. Publish the full version of your press release on your website, create supporting content that provides additional context, and develop multimedia assets that make your story more shareable. This foundation ensures that even if traditional media doesn't cover your announcement, your message still reaches your audience.

## Measuring Impact and Iterating Strategy

PR measurement extends far beyond counting media mentions or calculating advertising value equivalency. Effective measurement tracks how coverage influences business objectives: website traffic, lead generation, brand perception shifts, and ultimately revenue impact. Establishing clear metrics before launching campaigns enables you to evaluate success objectively and refine your approach based on evidence rather than assumptions.

Media monitoring tools track where your brand appears across publications, social media, and online forums. However, quantity of mentions matters less than quality and context. A single feature in a highly targeted industry publication often generates more business impact than dozens of mentions in general interest outlets. Analyze the sentiment, prominence, and audience alignment of each piece of coverage to understand true value.

Website analytics reveal how PR drives tangible business outcomes. Track referral traffic from media coverage, monitor conversion rates of visitors from different publications, and analyze how PR-driven traffic compares to other acquisition channels. This data informs future targeting decisions and helps justify PR investment to stakeholders focused on ROI.

Continuous improvement requires honest assessment of what works and what doesn't. Review each campaign to identify patterns: Which story angles generated the most interest? Which publications drove the highest-quality traffic? Which pitching approaches yielded the best response rates? Document these insights to build institutional knowledge that improves each subsequent campaign.

## Adapting to Emerging Trends

The PR landscape continues evolving as new platforms emerge, audience behaviors shift, and technological capabilities expand. Artificial intelligence now assists with media monitoring, content creation, and distribution optimization, but human judgment remains essential for strategic decision-making and relationship building. The most effective PR professionals leverage technology to handle repetitive tasks while focusing their expertise on strategy, creativity, and authentic relationship development.

Podcast appearances represent growing opportunities for thought leadership and brand building. As audio content consumption increases, securing guest spots on relevant podcasts provides extended opportunities to share your expertise, tell your story, and connect with engaged audiences. Research podcasts in your industry, understand their format and audience, and pitch episode ideas that provide genuine value to listeners.

Video content increasingly dominates digital media consumption, creating opportunities for visual storytelling that text alone cannot achieve. Develop video assets that complement your press releases: product demonstrations, executive interviews, customer testimonials, or behind-the-scenes content that humanizes your brand. These assets make your stories more appealing to digital-first publications and social media platforms.

The future of PR lies in integration—seamlessly blending traditional media relations with content marketing, social media strategy, influencer partnerships, and community building. Successful businesses recognize that PR is not a standalone function but a strategic component of comprehensive marketing that amplifies every other channel while building the credibility and trust that paid advertising cannot achieve.
